Frequently Asked Questions
Which IDE should I use to create my web pages?

An integrated development environment (IDE) is an essential tool for most web page developers. Though I usually use WebStorm and Geany, in this class we will probably use Visual Studio Code.

NOTE: I tend to make recommendations rather than force decisions on you. Though I don't recommend it, you may choose to spend the entire quarter working in just notepad++ or another tool such as Sublime Text, Aptana, Webstorm, Dreamweaver, Visual Studio Code, Brackets, or any number of programs out there.

If you are an experienced developer this will be an easy course for you. However, my experience is that most students in this course don't have a deep background writing code and find the material challenging. It is simplest for those students just to use the same tools I use. That way they can more easily immitate what I'm doing, and it is usually much easier for me to provide help. In some cases, if a student chooses a tool I don't know, then I will not be able to help them at all due to time constraints.
Can I get VS Code for free?

VS Code is open source and is always free. It is different than Visual Studio, and much better suited to our needs. We will discuss the install elsewhere.
How do I access my free Lynda.com account?
The Bellevue Web site explains how to access Lynda.com.
Can I submit work late?
I accept late work but at a price. Details to be explained elsewhere.
My house is burning down and I'm being attacked by orcs. May I submit the assignment a few days late?

For the midterm or final contact me immediately in the case of an emergancy to arrange alternative submission dates. I generally accept late submissions for other assignments, particularly if they are a bit tricky. If an assignment is turned in one to three days late it will cost you a few points and be no big deal, at least from my perspective. But assignments that are weeks late are hard for me to grade because I've forgotten what I was looking for on the assignment and how other students performed. In those cases, expect serious grade penalities.
May I take the test/quiz on a different day?
Please avoid rescheduling the quiz/tests as this creates a hardship for everyone involved.
When are your office hours?
I hope to be available for 1/2 hour after class on Mondays but this depends on factors beyond my control. Contact me to schedule another time if necessary. The best technique is to submit questions in the discussion area. In some cases we can meet online Thursday mornings via Google Hangouts. We will discuss this further in class. With very rare exceptions, never send technical questions to my personal or Canvas email. Use those tools only for discussing personal matters that are non technical. For instance, if you have a health issue or a crisis of some kind then you can use email to contact me. But nearly all technical questions go to either the discussion area or Google Hangouts.
Is there a tutor?
There may be tutors to help with programming available in the Academic Success Center (located in Bldg D204). This varies from quarter to quarter. If you are looking for tutoring assistance, the best place to start is by calling them at 425-564-2200 or check the website at http://bellevuecollege.edu/asc/tutoring.
I missed class last week. Did I miss anything important?
Some classes are more imporant than others, but each day will have new information. If you are unable to attend class then review the material on Canvas. For further information you will want to consult a fellow student or an online resource. In general, my policy is to allow each student to miss one class without comment. If you miss multiple classes then my sympathy for any potential hardships you may encounter might be affected. I am not heartless, but I'm prejudice in favor of students who attend class regularly.
I see that there are multiple Programming 109 classes being offered. May I sit on the other one?
Unfortunately Washington State and Bellevue College do not allow people to sit in on classes they are not enrolled in. Sitting in a different section of 109 is technically a different class. Also, space is often limited in the classes at this time. Therefore if you need extra class time please try to form a study group with other students. 
May I use a video camera, iPhone, or other device to record your lectures?
Perhaps. Yes if it is authorized by DRC (Disability Resource Center). Recording audio may be OK with me, but please check with me first. This is for several reasons including copyright of educational content (both my content and BC's), the privacy of other students in the class, and my privacy.  If you have permission of DRC then it is OK as long as you also agree not to post the recorded audio or video content to internet sites (public or private), or make any content available to anyone else in any format. Please understand that I want to be protective of my own image, and the images/audio of other students, floating around the Internet.

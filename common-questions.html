Frequent Questions & Answers

1. Which IDE should I use to create my web pages?

An IDE usually stands for integrated development environment. For this class it is a fancy way to say web page creator. In this course we will start off with notepad -- a bare bones editor available with MS Windows. After that -- even though you can design whole websites with nothing but a simple editor like notepad -- we will often work with notepad++ (because of all its user-friendly features). I will also use Brackets.

You may choose to spend the entire quarter working in just notepad or notepad++. You may also use another environment such as Sublime Text, Aptana, Webstorm, Dreamweaver, Visual Studio, Brackets, or any number of programs out there.

The most important thing is to not let it get in your way. I've actually had students not complete their assignments because they didn't know which application to use to create their web sites. Generally notepad++ or Brackets will work fine.

2. Can I get Visual Studio or Dreamweave for free?

Brackets is available for free. Adobe Dreamweaver is available at a discount to enrolled students. Please consult the adobe.com website for further information about ordering their product. A limited edition of Visual Studio is available for enrolled BC students through the DreamSpark WebStore. 

Another IDE that gets rave reviews is WebStorm (available at www.jetbrains.com). An academic license is available for $29 at this time.

10. May I use a video camera, iPhone, or other device to record your lectures?
I don't allow recording of video recording unless it is authorized by DRC (Disability Resource Center). Recording audio may be OK with me, but please check with me first. This is for several reasons including copyright of educational content (both my content and BC's), the privacy of other students in the class, and my privacy.  If you have permission of DRC then it is OK as long as you also agree not to post the recorded audio or video content to internet sites (public or private), or make any content available to anyone else in any format. Please understand that I want to be protective of my own image, and the images/audio of other students, floating around the Internet.
